Antiquity: Book 0 - "A Death in the Family"

endSequence

The cub's continued enthusiasm was remarkable.

"Mommy!"

A gentle, blue-white glow shot in front of me and into the ceiling. Lungs burning - I wasted no time in following him. 

I wished I had...

"Mom...my?" The cub was tentative - pausing almost dramatically as a sense of dread began permeating the waters around me. "What's wrong with Daddy?"

It was then I noticed. Drifting lifelessly in cloud of red was "Daddy" - a mountain of a man. Just over 8 feet in height his gargantuan frame floated - pierced from all angles with a dozen spears. Badly shredded clothing adorned his muscular, ebony form - his skin covered with marks made from bite upon bite dealt by the rodents whose lifeless bodies now floated - carpeting the ceiling. His forehead was caved in from a blow. Bruises and cuts marred his face - an expression of mixed anger and sorrow still apparent beneath.

And that's when I felt his disembodied hand brush against my side.

I screamed.

PANIC! Eyes wide I searched desperately for reprieve. I'd just expelled the last of my air - good or not. The next breath would be my last. For no other reason than my own determination did I survive - my now fevered mind begging the cub for his help.

As I'd hoped - he heard me. He flashed a pleading expression to his mother - who's face remained warm, and gentle. She smiled.

"Breathe, child."

Her voice was soft and caring. It was strong, and warm. I wanted more than anything to trust her - but I couldn't. I pressed against the floor of the chamber - mistakenly launching myself into the ceiling - headlong. The pain was intense - small amounts of my own blood now mingling with that of the Father's as I clutched at the new wound. Everything faded to darkness.

And then my eyes opened.

There was a flash - myself momentarily enveloped in the same, bluish-white glow of the cub. Then water... I felt water - cooling my lungs as it rushed in. 

"I'm drowning!"

There was nothing more I could do. Passing out - even for a second - was all my body needed to regain control over my breathing. The water was rushing in. I was out of air. I was dying. There was nothing more I could do. I gave up.

Then my body went limp - and I coughed. 

The offending fluid expelled partially from my lungs it rushed back in - beginning to flow in and out of my lungs as I coughed violently. Gathering myself mentally, I took a final deep breath and was finally calm. And breathing.

And amazed to be alive.

Oko grinned broadly. "Terra!" he screamed - jetting through the water on powerful forepaws. With an excited cry the cub struck my belly in a mock-hug. His enthusiasm was like a kid's at Christmas - each call of my name even more enthusiastic than the last. "Terra! Terra! Terra! Terra! Terra! Thank you, Mommy!"

I looked to Mommy - who simply smiled. It was as if I felt a tear in her eye - sadness weighing her heart. It was then that she spoke again - saddened and uncertain.

"The time has come where we must pass - and our burden must pass to you. Your inheritance ... is this."

From the floor in front of me lifted a book - unaffected by the waters around it. "Take it." she continued. Reaching out, I took the book - placing it into a leather pack the cub brought me in his teeth. "It was Daddy's." he whispered. I put the pack over my shoulder.

"Do not forsake the wisdom there, Terra. At times it will be your only salvation. And Oko?"

"Yes Mommy?"

She looked to me - a mischievous smile dancing playfully through her eyes and across her lips. "Take care of your sister."

Then the sensation started. It was a lurching sensation - making me sick to my stomach. I found myself in the ceremonial chamber again - the complicated lock sitting below me. My instincts not quite reasoning I headed for air - my stomach heaving. Water spewed out from my lungs. The water gone now I began to vomit - a horrible, inhuman sound expelling the water in my stomach. I ran to the bathroom - the convulsions continuing for several minutes before I managed to return to my bed - trembling and aching from the ordeal.

Sitting now on the edge of the bed I turned on my light and looked around me. I was awake now - and home. Water mixed with what little was left in my stomach lay splattered in the floor - marking my way to the bathroom. Everything else was in place however.

Everything except Oko.

I panicked - nervous - fear sprinkling itself lightly over aching muscles. My heart began to beat faster as I did another search of the room. My stuffed bear was gone!

Upset now at having lost my childhood friend I threw back the covers on my bed. The sound of children's laughter filled my ears - taunting me. He wasn't there. I started to think where he might be when the woman's final word began echoing hollowly  through my mind.

"SISTER!?"

Just then - something white began squirming its way out from under the bed - me staring in disbelief. Working himself free of his impromptu jailing the little ursine looked down at the floor - then up at me. A sense of concern filled me. Taking another few steps, Oko dragged the leather pack out from under the bed with him - both he and the pack still soaking wet from our ... adventure. He looked up at me again, locking eyes with me as his head tilted quizzically to one side.

"You OK, neechan?"

My mind ... wasn't coping with this. My chin dropped - I felt too horrible to be dreaming. Was I drugged somehow? I reached down - intending to pass my hand through the offending hallucination and reassure myself. Instead - I gave the poor thing a resounding slap.

Oko started back - looking hurt as he plopped back on his hind-quarters. Tears began welling in his eyes. Looking up at me again - his eyes begged me to be merciful.

"Nee... chan? What's wrong?"

And with that my eyes rolled back in my head - and I fainted.
